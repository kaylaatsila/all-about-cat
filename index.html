Definition

Cats, also called domestic cats (Felis catus), are small, carnivorous (meat-eating) mammals, of the family Felidae. There are lots of types, or breeds, of cats. Cats come in many different colors, sizes, with hair, without hair, and with or without tails. Most breeds—for example, the Abyssinian, the American Shorthair, and the Siamese—have short hair. Other breeds, including the Persian, have long, thick coats.

History

Ancient Egyptians worshipped cats as gods, and often mummified them so they could be with their owners "for all of eternity." The Egyptians probably domesticated, or tamed, wild cats about 3,500 years ago. They used these cats to kill the rats and mice that ate their stored grain.
